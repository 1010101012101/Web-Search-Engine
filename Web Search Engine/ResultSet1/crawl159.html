THE RUNDOWN BLOG | Dec. 9, 2013

Pope Francis most talked-about topic on Facebook in 2013

  • New data from Facebook revealed the year's most talked-about topics and trends on the social network. Pope Francis led the list, followed broadly by the word "election" and the Royal Baby. Facebook measured the number of posts in 2013 that mentioned a specific topic and then ranked those topics.

Robert MacNeil remembers the 1963 gunshots that killed President Kennedy

It's not cool to use slurs online, even if you're 'jk'

  • In a shift in attitude, most young people now say it's wrong to use racist or sexist slurs online, even if you're just kidding. But when they see them, they don't take much personal offense.

5 journalists who got their start covering the JFK assassination

REPORT | Nov. 12, 2013

Did 60 Minutes do enough to regain viewer trust after false Benghazi report?

  • News program 60 Minutes admitted it made a mistake in a segment about the U.S. consulate attack in Benghazi, which included false reports by a supposed eyewitness. Kelly McBride of the Poynter Institute and Tom Rosenstiel of the American Press Institute join Jeffrey Brown to critique the reporting and response.

How does Twitter's potential for profit measure up to its high stock price?

Hari Sreenivasan talks to financier Bill Hambrecht and USA Today's Alistair Barr about Twitter's potential for growth and the scrutiny that company executives still face.

Ifill and Woodruff rep the NewsHour on 'The Colbert Report'

Will Facebook change lead to an erosion of privacy rights for teens?

  • Privacy settings on Facebook will now allow users aged 13-17 to share posts with people outside their friend network. Hari Sreenivasan speaks with Stephen Balkam of the Family Online Safety Institute and Jim Steyer of Common Sense Media.
