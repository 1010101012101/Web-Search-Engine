How the Radio Connection Works

PRIVATE TUTORING/ONE-ON-ONE INSTRUCTION

Once you’re accepted into the program, you’ll attend class inside a real radio broadcasting facility in the city/town where you live (or another locale, if you so choose). You receive private, one-on-one instruction from a highly experienced radio professional, someone who is actually making a living in radio. You will follow our structured course curriculum (including homework), and you will get plenty of hands-on instruction in the studio. With our unique program, you do not spend a single day in a classroom! The radio station is your “classroom”, and a real radio professional is your teacher/mentor. And you are the only student in the class!

AN APPRENTICESHIP, NOT AN INTERNSHIP

Let’s make an important distinction here: our program is not an internship like other radio schools might offer, nor are we charging tuition for you to be an intern. With the Radio Connection, you are an apprentice, NOT an intern.

What is the difference? Interns are essentially people who run errands and do chores, and MAYBE get to learn a few things if there is nothing more pressing to do. An APPRENTICE IS A STUDENT, NOT A GOPHER. As an apprentice, you are in the radio station for the purpose of learning the ropes directly from a professional. Chores and errands are a normal part of the day-to-day operations of the radio station, but make no mistake: your tuition is paying for a personalized APPRENTICESHIP with a seasoned radio professional.

Interns are there to work; apprentices are there to learn. Interns get to add a little snippet to their resume; apprentices get hired, because they are trained by the people doing the hiring! See the difference?

At the Radio Connection, we believe our job is not just to get you through our program; our job is to PREPARE YOU FOR A CAREER IN RADIO. To be absolutely clear: NO. What is more, you should not trust ANY school that would make a guarantee like that. There are no guarantees in life, and certainly not in the competitive radio market. Only scams make bloated promises they can’t keep. As an accredited radio school with high standards of integrity and excellence, we do not make such promises.

Certainly it does.

So if getting close to the action increases your odds of success, doesn’t it make more sense to learn the radio business inside a real broadcasting facility than being stuck in a college or trade school classroom?

And does it not make the most sense to be trained one-on-one by a real radio professional—someone who can not only teach you, but who has the potential to become a great ally for you on the inside?

We think you know the answer.

If you are serious about a career in radio, and if you have the “street smarts” we talked about earlier, then you know there is no better place to put yourself than inside a real radio station as an apprentice, learning one-on-one from a professional. There is just no better option.

So, no, we won’t guarantee you a job. What we will promise is that between our on-the-job apprenticeships and our Stay Connected Program, we can put you in a better position to land a job than most of our competitors can. Making the most of that opportunity is up to you.
