Braxton Health Care Center under new ownership

With a seamless transition, Braxton Heath Care and Rehabilitation Center recently underwent a change in ownership. Sometime in October, American Medical Facilities Management, LLC , under the auspices of president and founder John Elliott, became the owners of the local facility.
The transaction actually came about, Brandon Totten, Community Relations Manager for AMFM, explained, when the previous owner “wanted to spend more time with his family.” According to Totten, he and Elliott, president of AMFM, are both members of WV Heath Care Association and “have known each other for quite some time.” Stressing that AMFM is West Virginia owned and operated, he explained that, with the acquisition of the Braxton facility, the company now owns and operates 12 such care centers located throughout the state.

For Graftons, Christmas trees are year round labor of love

Christmas in a small town can be both magical and memorable as family and friends carry on holiday traditions. Ed Grafton and his wife Willa make no exception to these traditions. In fact, they work hard year round to help keep Christmas alive. Together, the couple own and operate Berry Fork Enterprises in Heaters, where they grow a variety of Christmas trees. Although the farm is a large chapter in Grafton’s life, he has certainly had several epic experiences leading to his retirement.
Ed Grafton was born and raised in Fayette County, in the suburbs of Lookout. He then traveled to West Virginia University where he earned a Masters Degree in Forestry. After his studies were complete, Grafton became a member of the staff at his Alma Mater where he taught Forestry for two and a half years..

Commission announces Day Report Center reopening

At the conclusion of their regular meeting last Friday, County Commission members announced that the Braxton County Day Report Center would reopen. The decision came at a special meeting of the Community Corrections Board held in Lewis County on December 2. A large delegation represented Braxton County. They included Judge Rick Facemire, Magistrates Beth Smith and Rob Hamil, Prosecuting Attorney Kelly Hamon McLaughlin, Sheriff Eddie Williams and all three County Commissioners. “There was a lot of misunderstanding on both sides,” explained Commission President Gary Ellyson. “We are very pleased with the outcome and look forward to getting the center reopened,” he added.

 

Wright heads to jail, others receive probation

William Lester appeared recently in Braxton County Circuit Court representing Willaim Wright, along with Braxton County Prosecuting Attorney Kelly Hamon McLaughlin, for sentencing. It was reported to the Court that Wright had been returned to the local court due to failure to comply with the terms and conditions of the program set forth by the rehabilitation facility he had previously been permitted to attend.
Lester requested that Wright receive alternative sentencing and Prosecuting Attorney McLaughlin stood silent on the matter pursuant to the plea agreement. After due consideration, Judge Richard A. Facemire found that as a result of his drug addiction, the defendant has lost everything, but has taken no decisive action to address and overcome his addiction. Furthermore, the Court gave Wright the opportunity to correct his drug addiction and he failed to comply with the terms and conditions of long-term in-patient rehabilitation and was discharged from the program. The Court found that if granted probation or home confinement, Wright is likely to re-offend.

GSC ROTC Cadet excells at competition

A Glenville State College ROTC student has made history in the programs’ battalion. GSC senior Marc Jones has received the first excellent score for the ROTC Yellow Jacket Battalion since 2009. The accomplishment occurred during the program’s Leader Development and Assessment Course (LDAC) in Fort Lewis, Washington during 29 days of testing between the cadets’ junior and senior years. The point-based camp accommodated 5,700 cadets from 273 host schools around the country.

High School gym to host winter sports

Braxton County High athletic director Lynn Stalnaker and coaches of the winter sports teams are currently adapting to the use of the high-school gym instead of the Braxton County Armory facility for all basketball games and for the “Big 16” wrestling tournament coming up later in the season.
According to the AD, the Armory floor was flooded in September, what he termed “the worst it’s ever been,” and cannot be used. In discussing the problem, Stalnaker commented, “I feel sure it won’t be finished until the season’s over.” With that in mind, he and the basketball coaches have “worked out a schedule which is fair to everyone.”
