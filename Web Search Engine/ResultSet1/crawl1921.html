WCN photo by Diane Pottorff

Ceremony honors Pearl Harbor

By DIANE POTTORFF
Staff Writer

HUNTINGTON – It was 72 years ago on Dec. 7 the Imperial Japanese Navy surprised residents in Hawaii with a blitz attack on Pearl Harbor.

The attack destroyed or crippled many ships that were anchored in the Naval Base during the early morning hours of that Sunday.

Members of the Marine Corps League Detachment 340 of Huntington have not forgotten the sacrifice of those sailors and soldiers; remembering them each year with a ceremony in the Big Sandy Superstore Arena, followed by a wreath throwing into the Ohio River at Harris Riverfront Park.

Commission gets presentation on mine reclamation

By DIANE POTTORFF
Staff Writer

WAYNE – Officials with the Office of Coalfield Community Development, a program of the West Virginia Department of Energy in conjunction with the Rahall Transportation Institute, have been conducting a study on post mine land use.

The preliminary study for Wayne County was presented to the Wayne County Commission during its meeting Monday by Rachel Lester of the Energy Development office and officials with the Wayne County Economic Development Authority.

“It is a development tool to identify former mining sites for future economic development,” said Wayne County Administrator Jim Boggs.

HONORED – Ceredo Fire Chief David Caudill pins a medal to the uniform of Firefighter Russell Hutchinson while Mayor Otis Adkins looks on. Hutchinson was honored for his 60 years of service to the Ceredo Volunteer Fire Department. WCN photo by Diane Pottorff

Fire department honors long-time member

By DIANE POTTORFF
Staff Writer

KENOVA – Russell “Hutch” Hutchinson has been an important part of the Ceredo Volunteer Fire Department since the beginning of the department.

Hutchinson was honored by his fellow firefighters Friday during their annual Christmas dinner in the Kenova United Methodist Church Fellowship Hall for his 60 years of service, as the officers and members presented him the first Distinguished Fire Service award.

Hutchinson began providing emergency assistance to the Ceredo community when he was 15 years old, he said. He is considered to be the final charter member of the fire department that is still active. Ceredo VFD was chartered in 1959.

Presentation on drug abuse Dec. 12

KENOVA –Tim White, Wayne County Anti-Drug Coalition and Prevention coordinator, will conduct a presentation on the use and abuse of marijuana and heroin at the Kenova Police Community Meeting set for 6 p.m. Thursday at Kenova Town Hall.

Topics to be discussed include: the physical effects of marijuana and heroin; the long-term effects of those drugs; the issues surrounding the medical marijuana debate; and the prevalence of marijuana and heroin in the Tri-State.

There will also be a panel discussion following the presentation featuring representatives from law enforcement, the mental health field and county schools, as well as child advocates, legislators, medical personnel and social workers.

DEU has another successful year

By DIANE POTTORFF
Staff Writer

WAYNE – For the third straight year, the Wayne County Sheriff’s Drug Enforcement Unit has maintained a conviction rate of 100 percent for those dealing or transporting drugs into Wayne County.

According to spokesman Lt. Mark Bradshaw, from Oct. 1, 2012 to Oct. 1, 2013, the DEU made 45 arrests of those suspected of possessing illegal drugs after serving 145 felony warrants, five misdemeanor warrants and 14 search warrants.

Officers with the unit conducted 21 heroin buys and busted four methamphetamine labs, Bradshaw said. Also during this time, the unit seized $29,557 in cash and seized one vehicle.
