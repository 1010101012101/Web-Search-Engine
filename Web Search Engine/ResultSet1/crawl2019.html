Lennox appoints new Finance Officer

Lennox City Council opened their meeting with Webelos Pack 109 presenting the colors.
Mayor Orville Wiebers thanked the scouts for coming to the meeting.
Following the approval of the minutes, claims, payroll and finance officer’s report, the council held the second reading of Ordinance 539, an ordinance to rezone the property at 905 S. Main St. from residential to general business. The ordinance was approved.
The next item of business was the second reading of Ordinance 540, an ordinance that would rezone the old football field property from NRC Floodplain/Conservation District to General Business District.
Resident Don Kniffen reiterated his opposition to the ordinance. “Adjacent landowners don’t think this is a good fit for our community,” he said.

Superintendent search underway

The Lennox School District 41-4 board of education held their December meeting Monday night at 6 p.m. The Lennox High School and LWC Middle School concert was also scheduled for this night at 8 p.m. The board approved the financial reports as presented.
Superintendent Robert Mayer updated the board on the Planning Time Task force, saying the group had met three times and would be presenting a proposal to the board at the January meeting. Mayer requested the board to email any questions the members may have to him ahead of time so the task force would be able to address any issues.

Journey to family: Local couple doubly blessed through adoption

Becoming parents
Dave and Kimberly Klock knew they wanted to become parents after they married. They planned to have three children. The couple’s quest for children took them on a journey neither expected but both embraced.
After unsuccessful attempts to conceive, the couple turned to adoption. When asked about their preferences, the Klocks placed few restrictions on their adoption application. Like most parents to be, they simply hoped for a healthy child to love.

Lennox School District 41-1 asks for input in superintendent search

The Lennox School District Board of Education has selected Dakota Education Consulting (DEC) to assist the district in its selection of the next Superintendent of Schools for the Lennox School District.  The selection process will begin in earnest in January of 2014 but prior to that the Board of Education has asked DEC to gather data on the desirable qualities of the next Superintendent. The survey link below is designed to gather that information.  Please take a few minutes and complete the survey.  Your responses will be anonymous and will advise the Board on the important characteristics of the next leader of the Lennox School District.  The survey will be open through Dec. 8.
Visit this link online at: https://www.surveymonkey.com/s/69MRF93

Residents speak out against rezoning of old football field property

Following a public hearing on the rezoning of the “old football field” property, it was the consensus of the Lennox Council to not rezone the property to General Business District, due to the concerns brought by residents living in the neighborhood.
Lennox Properties, LLC had purchased the property from the Lennox School District. The sale was disputed by the City of Lennox, because the City had a right of first refusal on the property which gave them an opportunity to match any bids on the property. However, because the property was sold in combination with the old school administration building, it was the opinion of the City that the sale was not valid. The City also had a use agreement with the school that allowed them to use the property for another ten years. The City has taken legal action to retain the property.

School board members approved Architecture Incorporated’s plan for the 7th and 8th grade addition at the high school and to develop bid documents based on this plan at their special meeting held last Thursday.

Chancellor school property sold

The former Chancellor School building was sold at Monday night’s school board meeting, the high bid was accepted from Lynn Eining of Lennox in the amount of $13,500. The two lots adjacent to the school building were also sold, as the board accepted the bid from Chris Bartmann, of Chancellor in the amount of $6,500 total for the two lots.
In other business Superintendent Robert Mayer recognized the achievements of Sara Bakken, who is in her first year of teaching 6th through 8th grade band in the Lennox District, as well as assisting with the high school marching band in the District. Bakken was recently awarded the SDMEA Outstanding Young Music Educator Award. Mayer said it was a great honor as the award is voted on by her peers.

Tony Groebner honored for service to LADC

At the last Lennox Area Development Corporation (LADC) meeting, President Jeff Elhoff presented a plaque to treasurer Tony Groebner to recognize him for 26 years of outstanding service to the board.  There was coffee and cake provided after the meeting to thank Groebner and two other out-going members—Elhoff and Mike Fjerstad.
Groebner was asked to replace outgoing treasurer Bud Wilson 26 years ago and he has continued to serve as treasurer since then. Groebner said that the board never seemed to have any money until they started receiving 1/2 of the second penny sales tax. They used that money to buy and develop the industrial park land. They also built a spec building.

Drug incident at LWC middle school

An incident at the middle school last week resulted in one student taken into custody and several others disciplined.  Middle School Principal Chad Allison said they received a report last week that a student had given another student or students pills.

State Highway 17 slated for major improvements in 2016

The South Dakota Department of Transportation hosted an open house last week to give local residents an overview of the planned SD Highway 17 project and to receive input from the public. The project is planned for 2016.
Mark Leiferman, Chief Road Design Engineer, presented a power point outlining the project. The improvements will begin at the City limits north of Lennox and continue to the junction of Highway 44 at the south edge of Lennox.

 To read the entire story, subscribe. 

Lewis & Clark capital call rejected

The Lewis & Clark Regional Water System’s board of directors voted Oct. 24 to reject the capital call, which would have required LCRWS members to put more money into the project to keep it moving forward. Lack of federal funds has stalled the project and members had two options: continue the “wait and see” strategy and hope federal funds would be delivered, or to ask members that have already fully paid for their portions to pay more into the system.
Five of 20 members — Luverne, Worthington, Lincoln Pipestone Rural Water System, Lennox and Beresford — voted in favor of the capital call. Lennox Council instructed their representative to vote for the capital call, citing that the best thing for LCRWS members would be to get all members on the system as soon as possible.
