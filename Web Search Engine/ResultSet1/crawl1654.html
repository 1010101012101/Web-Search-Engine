NEW KIDS COUNT POLICY REPORT

The Annie E. Casey Foundation has released its latest KIDS COUNT policy report ? The First Eight Years: Giving Kids a Foundation for Lifetime Success. The report  details how a child’s early development across critical areas of well-being is essential to make the transition into elementary school and into a thriving adulthood. The report contains never-before-reported information from a longitudinal study that began to track children in kindergarten in 1998-99, detailing how well they were on track by third grade in key developmental measures.

In addition to the new policy report, the KIDS COUNT Data Center is a one-stop source for child well-being data, featuring the KIDS COUNT overall rankings, geographic profiles and customizable maps, graphs and rankings for use in publications and presentations. The regularly updated data cover the national, state, county, city and congressional and school district that will offer a variety of ways to access data on hundreds of indicators of child well-being at the state, city and community level, as well as information on racial and ethnic groups, including children in immigrant families.
