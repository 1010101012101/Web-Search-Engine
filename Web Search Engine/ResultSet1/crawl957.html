Praise Jesus and - If You Really Want to Go There - Satan, Lord Hanuman, Muhammad, Selassie, Adonai and Social Justice

In an engaging cluster-f#*k of political rhetoric meeting constitutional legality, Oklahoma's privately funded, GOP-sponsored state capitol monument to the 10 Commandments, already facing an ACLU lawsuit, now faces another challenge - a proposed neighboring monument "in good taste" to Satan, and maybe a "big and weather-proof" one to the Hindu god Lord Hanuman. Appalled (and oblivous) Oklahoma legislators argue we are "a faith-based nation." Exactly, say Satanists, activists who view their primarily political stance as a “poison pill” in the Church/State debate and who argue in the name of "a rebel angel defiant of autocratic structure." Hindus have now joined in the fracas, as have gleeful atheists who call Oklahoma's constitutional dilemma "beautiful."

-Abby Zimet

On The Edge of the Line: L.A. Sheriff's Deputies Indicted For Longstanding Violent Abuse

After years of damning charges, rumors and reports, over a dozen officers at the notorious Los Angeles Sheriff’s Department have been indicted for brutal abuse of jail inmates ranging from fracture-causing beatings to falsifying records to assaulting visitors. The indictments echo charges from the FBI, the ACLU, a county commission and news stories that deputies were regularly encouraged to beat inmates, but "not in the face." The grand jury said Sheriff Lee Baca was responsible for "behavior that had become institutionalized." Baca said "it's a sad day" when people are saying all these mean things about him.

No Justice, No Piece: Domino's Fires Workers Seeking Living Wage Because, What, Do They Expect A Living Wage?

A Domino's Pizza in New York City has reportedly fired 20 employees who joined last week's nationwide fast food protests after they complained about unfair wages and lousy working conditions. The delivery workers - who are paid under $6 an hour and rely heavily on tips - had been asked to work extended hours inside, with no tips or raise to minimum wage. One of many NYC companies accused of various forms of wage theft, Domino's has already faced worldwide protests and charges of illegally firings. And their pizza is gross.

12.09.13 - 11:52 PM
Surveillance Is Theft: World's Leading Authors Protest NSA

Calling it "a stand for democracy in the digital age," 560 of the world's most renowned writers, including five Nobel prize winners, have signed a petition condemning state surveillance and urging the U.N. to create an international bill of digital rights. The authors, whose appeal is being published in over 30 newspapers and can be signed by the public, says the surveillance revealed by Edward Snowden violates privacy, compromises freedom of thought and undermines the right of us all to remain "unobserved and unmolested."

"A person under surveillance is no longer free; a society under surveillance is no longer a democracy."

On Netanyahu's Revisionism and Burying the Past Through Praise

As world leaders seeking absolution conspicuously mourn and praise Nelson Mandela, astute observers are increasingly calling out those whose own governments either did too little to get Mandela out of prison or too much to support the regime that put him there for 27 years. The complicit list - from Thatcher and Reagan to Clinton and Peres - is topped by Benjamin Netanyahu, who in "the most shameless piece of historical revisionism" has proclaimed Mandela "a freedom fighter who disavowed violence" - wrong, wrong on too many counts to count.
