Veteran Vancouver journalist Andy Ross dies at 66
Long-time Vancouver Province editor Andy Ross has passed away at age 66 after a short illness. Read more »
Celebrity Gossip: Guilty Pleasure or Real News?
On November 28, the Canadian Journalism Foundation hosted a journalism talk in Toronto on the newsworthiness of celebrity gossip and how entertainment coverage is evolving in the mainstream media. Representatives from The Globe and Mail, Toronto Star, National Post, Hello! Canada and CTV’s e-talk shared their insights on some of the unique challenges of covering famous faces in the news, and offered their thoughts on the newsworthiness of celebrity journalism. Read more »
Start preparing your entries for the Goff Penny Awards
The annual awards program is open to young journalists between the ages of 20-25 working for CNA-member daily newspapers. Young freelance journalists and student interns whose work has been published in a CNA-member newspaper are also eligible to enter. Read more »
Additional credit tools available for Newspapers Canada members
Ross Edmunds, credit analyst for Newspapers Canada, has developed a series of PowerPoint presentations on credit issues facing newspapers. The third PowerPoint presentation is titled "Creating a Credit is Everyone’s Business Program" and is now available for our newspaper members. Read more »
Next webinar highlights better ways to use audience measurement data
Join us on Tuesday, December 17 for a unique training session on audience measurement data with Melanie Goddard from KubasPrimedia. Goddard will cover the importance of relevant and accurate audience measurement, discuss what's right and wrong with today's measurement strategies and address the future of measurement. Read more »

TC Media acquires Sun Media’s community papers in Quebec
TC Media, a division of TC Transcontinental, and Sun Media Corporation, a subsidiary of Quebecor Media, announce that they have signed a final agreement under which the 74 Sun Media community papers and associated web properties in Quebec will be acquired by TC Media for an amount of $75 million.
